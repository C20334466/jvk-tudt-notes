Types of Data Analysis

Descriptive

Summarisation and description of data, using statistical measures and visualisation.

  • Measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode)
  • Measures of variability (variance, standard deviation)
  • Visualisation of distribution (barchart, histogram, boxplot, probability density curve)

Exploratory

A look at connections that are not immediately obvious, that is, correlations between variables.

  • Pearson's correlation coefficient
  • Kendall's Tau
  • Chi-square test
  • ANOVA
  • Information gain
  • Lift

Inferential

Drawing conclusions about populations based on samples.

  • Estimation of population parameters from samples (all the measures under 'Descriptive')
  • Confidence intervals
  • Hypothesis testing

Predictive

Building of models from known data for the prediction of unknown data values.

Causal

[Not treated in this module]

Prediction of what happens to one variable if another changes.

Mechanistic

[Not treated in this module]

Even more exact than causal. Looks to explain the mechanism of causation between two variables.
